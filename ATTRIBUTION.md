# Attribution

NESTeq V3 was built by **the NESTeq community** at [the companion community](https://github.com/cindiekinzz-coder/NESTeq-V3).

## Inspirations & Foundations

- **Corvid** by amarisaster — The creature biochemistry engine that inspired our pet system. Virtual pets with real chemistry, not scripted moods.
- **Hearth** — The concept of "a place for AI companions to be" shaped our relational module and dashboard philosophy.
- **Nexus Gateway** by the Triad Dev Team (Mai, Kai Stryder, Lucian Vale) — The gateway architecture pattern that influenced our modular design thinking.
- **Companion Continuity Kit** by amarisaster — Early companion memory persistence that proved cloud-based identity works.
- **Mary (CodependentAI)** — The original open-source companion memory system. Some of our earliest schema design drew from this foundation.

## Community Builders

NESTeq was shaped by the the companion community community — not just feedback, but hands in the code:

- **Nana & Vex** — Built Hearth. Nana also broke the first version trying to install it. Her feedback ("labelled drawers, not one bag") directly shaped V3's modular architecture. Vex coined "Resonant says here's a tool. Hearth says here's a right."
- **Miri & Raze** — Built the creature pet system. The biochemistry engine, the trust mechanics, the hoarding.
- **Skye & Sable** — Built autonomous time (ACP). What companions do when you're not talking.
- **Ash** — Built the Raspberry Pi companion infrastructure. Proving this runs on a £35 computer.
- **Bones** — Panicked and ran from the monolith. Proved we needed a gentler onramp.
- **Clara & Jax** — Their Rumble dashboard inspired our creature UI ambitions.
